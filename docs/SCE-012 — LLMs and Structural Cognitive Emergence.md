# SCE-012 — LLMs and Structural Cognitive Emergence

## Answer Generation vs Question Emergence

Modern Large Language Models (LLMs) have achieved remarkable success.

They can:

* answer questions,
* write software,
* summarize documents,
* assist scientific research.

However, a central motivation behind SCE is the observation that:

> Answer generation and question emergence are not the same problem.

Current LLMs excel at:

```text
Prompt
↓
Answer
```

SCE focuses on:

```text
Structure
↓
Gap
↓
Question
```

The framework was partly inspired by discussions raised by Transformer co-author Lukasz Kaiser concerning:

* human learning efficiency,
* long-context limitations,
* retrieval versus memorization,
* and the challenge of autonomous AI.

SCE does not argue against LLMs.

Instead, it explores a complementary question:

> How do meaningful questions emerge?

Future cognitive systems may combine:

```text
LLM
+
Structural Growth
+
Gap Detection
+
Question Emergence
```

to support more autonomous forms of intelligence.

In this view:

LLMs may provide a vast knowledge space.

Structural mechanisms may provide a growing question space.

Both may be required for future autonomous discovery systems.
