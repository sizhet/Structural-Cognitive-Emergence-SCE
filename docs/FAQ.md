# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## Q1. Is SCE a neuroscience theory?

No.

SCE is primarily a structural cognitive framework.

It focuses on organizational principles rather than biological implementation details.

## Q2. Is SCE an AI architecture?

Not directly.

SCE is a conceptual framework describing:

- concept emergence,
- question emergence,
- cognitive growth,
- autonomous inquiry.

Future AI architectures may be inspired by these ideas.

## Q3. Is SCE opposed to LLMs?

No.

SCE does not argue against LLMs.

Instead, SCE explores a complementary problem:

    Answer Generation

versus

    Question Emergence

Future systems may benefit from both.

## Q4. Does SCE explain consciousness?

No.

SCE focuses on:

- concepts,
- questions,
- curiosity,
- exploration.

Consciousness remains a separate and much larger topic.

## Q5. Why start with cats?

Because concept emergence is easier to observe than consciousness.

The Cat Problem provides a simple and intuitive entry point into deeper cognitive questions.

## Q6. Why are children important in SCE?

Children naturally exhibit:

- rapid concept formation,
- high curiosity,
- high question density.

These properties make childhood a valuable window into cognitive growth.

## Q7. What is a Gap?

A Gap is not simple absence.

A Gap is a structured incompleteness inside a cognitive structure.

Questions emerge when such gaps become visible.

## Q8. What is Question Density?

Question Density refers to the frequency with which a cognitive system generates meaningful questions from its structures.

SCE proposes that Question Density may be an important indicator of cognitive growth.

## Q9. What is Cross-Graph Gap Emergence?

Many important discoveries may emerge not from a single cognitive graph, but from interactions between multiple graphs.

Examples include:

- interdisciplinary science,
- entrepreneurship,
- human-AI collaboration,
- future multi-brain AI systems.

## Q10. What is the central idea of SCE?

The entire framework can be summarized as:

    Structure
    ↓
    Gap
    ↓
    Question
    ↓
    Exploration
    ↓
    Structure

Concepts emerge from structures.

Questions emerge from gaps.

Intelligence grows through the interaction of both.
