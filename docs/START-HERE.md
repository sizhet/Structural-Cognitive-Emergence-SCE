# START HERE — Structural Cognitive Emergence (SCE)

Welcome to **Structural Cognitive Emergence (SCE)**.

This repository studies how structural cognition may emerge from:

* **sparse examples** rather than large brute-force datasets,
* **structural gaps** rather than only direct question-answer supervision,
* and **developmental feedback** rather than static memorization alone.

At the center of the repository are a few recurring ideas:

* **The Cat Problem** — how a concept may emerge from only a few structurally informative examples.
* **Gap / Why mechanisms** — how missing structure gives rise to meaningful why-questions.
* **Calling Graphs, Differential Trees, and Triggering Links** — a three-layer structural substrate for relation, category, and action-trigger knowledge.
* **In-situ knowledge growth** — how structural knowledge can grow through interaction, mismatch, and feedback.
* **Structural Cognitive Runtime (SCR)** — the new runtime phase of the project, where emergence mechanisms are organized into a runtime architecture for **ASI Brain Units** and **Brain Unit Core**.

This page is a **reading guide**, not a full summary.
Its goal is simple: help you enter the repository from the right door.

# What SCR Adds to SCE

The earlier SCE line mainly asks:

> **How can structural cognition emerge?**

The new runtime phase asks an additional question:

> **How should structural cognition actually run?**

That question leads to **Structural Cognitive Runtime (SCR)**—a runtime architecture built around:

* structural memory,
* structural localization,
* sparse concept formation,
* gap / why generation,
* developmental update,
* and decision / action / escalation interfaces.

SCR also introduces a distinction between:

* **front-end ASI Brain Units**, and
* a larger back-end **Brain Unit Core**.
