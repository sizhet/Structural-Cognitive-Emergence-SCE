# SCE-015 — STRUCTURAL COGNITIVE RUNTIME: THE TWIN OF KNOWLEDGE AND MECHANISM

## Abstract

Structural Cognitive Emergence (SCE) has progressively revealed that cognition cannot be fully explained by accumulated knowledge alone.

A child, an animal, a scientist, or a future Autonomous Structural Intelligence (ASI) system may possess different amounts of knowledge, yet all exhibit similar patterns of reasoning, question generation, gap discovery, explanation construction, and adaptive learning.

This observation suggests that cognition consists of two inseparable components:

- Structural Knowledge
- Structural Runtime

Structural Knowledge represents what is known.

Structural Runtime represents how knowledge is activated, expanded, evaluated, explained, and updated.

This work proposes a Structural Cognitive Runtime model and argues that cognition emerges not from knowledge alone, but from the continuous interaction between structural knowledge and structural runtime.

## 1. Introduction

Many theories of intelligence focus primarily on knowledge.

Examples include:

- facts
- concepts
- memories
- rules
- experiences

However, a fundamental question remains:

> Why can two individuals possessing similar knowledge exhibit dramatically different reasoning abilities?

The answer may lie in the distinction between knowledge and mechanism.

Knowledge alone does not reason.

Knowledge alone does not ask questions.

Knowledge alone does not discover gaps.

Something must continuously operate upon knowledge.

SCE refers to this operating process as Structural Cognitive Runtime.

## 2. Knowledge Is Not Cognition

Consider a newborn infant.

The infant possesses very little explicit knowledge.

Yet the infant already demonstrates:

- attention
- exploration
- curiosity
- preference formation
- feedback adaptation

This suggests that cognition cannot depend entirely upon stored knowledge.

The runtime must exist before significant knowledge accumulation occurs.

In this sense:

    Knowledge can be sparse.
    
    Runtime cannot be absent.

## 4. Structural Runtime

Knowledge structures alone do not explain cognition.

A runtime must continuously operate upon those structures.

Current SCE evidence suggests the following runtime sequence.

### Step 1: Locate

Locate relevant nodes.

Example:

    Observed Animal
        ↓
    Cat Node

### Step 2: Expand

Expand surrounding structures.

    Cat
        ↓
    Mammal
    Pet
    Predator
    Domestic Animal

### Step 3: Trigger

Activate related behavioral and decision structures.

    Hungry
        ↓
    Food Seeking

### Step 4: Score

Aggregate evidence.

    Evidence
        ↓
    Scoring Tree

### Step 5: Decide

Select the strongest structural interpretation.

### Step 6: Explain

Detect structural gaps.

Generate:

    Why?

and

    How?

### Step 7: Update

Modify structures using feedback.

This completes one runtime cycle.

## 6. Why Questions Emerge

One of the most important consequences of this runtime is the emergence of questions.

Traditional systems often treat questions as language outputs.

SCE proposes a different view.

Questions emerge when runtime encounters unresolved structures.

Examples:

    Missing Node
    
    Missing Relation
    
    Missing Trigger
    
    Missing Evidence

These become:

    Why?
    
    How?
    
    What if?
    
    What next?

Question generation is therefore a natural consequence of runtime operation.

## 7. Gap Why as Runtime Activity

Previous SCE studies demonstrated:

This interpretation fits naturally into the runtime framework.

The runtime continuously compares:

    Expected Structure

with

    Observed Structure

Differences produce gaps.

Gaps generate explanations.

Thus Why generation is not a special language capability.

It is a runtime function.

## 8. Infant Learning and Runtime Primacy

Infants provide a powerful clue regarding the origin of cognition.

An infant possesses:

- limited concepts
- limited memories
- limited language

Yet exhibits:

- exploration
- adaptation
- preference formation
- attention shifts

This suggests:

    Runtime precedes knowledge scale.

Knowledge accumulates later.

The runtime already exists.

Thus infant cognition may be one of the clearest windows into the fundamental architecture of intelligence.

### 10. The Twin Principle

The central proposal of this work is the Twin Principle.

Cognition always consists of two inseparable components.

    Structural Knowledge

and

    Structural Runtime

Knowledge provides structure.

Runtime provides activity.

Knowledge provides memory.

Runtime provides cognition.

Neither alone is sufficient.

Together they produce intelligence.

### Conclusion

Structural Cognitive Emergence increasingly suggests that intelligence cannot be understood solely through accumulated knowledge.

Beneath concepts, memories, categories, and relations lies a continuously operating runtime responsible for localization, expansion, triggering, evaluation, explanation, and adaptation.

This leads to a simple but powerful formulation:

    Structural Cognition
    =
    Structural Knowledge
    +
    Structural Runtime

The distinction between knowledge and mechanism may represent one of the most important conceptual foundations for future studies of human cognition, infant learning, scientific discovery, education, and Autonomous Structural Intelligence.
